PUPPY SHARE DEALS Puppy share is very different -- it's a CO-OWNERSHIP arrangement of a dog. How it works: You own a quality male or female You place that dog with another breeder or show person You BOTH own the dog -- shared ownership When breeding happens, you both benefit from the puppies You typically get "pick of litter" or a percentage of each litter The dog may live with the other person while you retain breeding rights Why breeders do this: You have limited space or resources but want to keep your genetics producing. A puppy share lets you expand your program without raising every litter yourself. You stay invested in the dog's future. Key point: Puppy share is ongoing co-ownership. You're partners in that dog's production. THE CRITICAL DIFFERENCE Stud fee = One transaction. You get paid once. It's done. Puppy share = Ongoing partnership. You both own the dog. You both profit from future litters. It continues until the arrangement ends. REAL TALK Both arrangements require WRITTEN CONTRACTS. I cannot stress this enough. Get everything in writing: Who owns breeding rights Who gets pick of litter How expenses are split What happens if the dog gets injured What happens if the partnership ends Who can breed to the dog and when I've seen beautiful friendships destroyed and legitimate breeding programs derailed because people handshook a deal and assumed they understood each other. They didn't. The other thing -- be VERY careful with puppy share arrangements in countries where legal protection is limited. Make sure you understand the laws around shared ownership in your area.
